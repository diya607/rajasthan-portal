Research-based System Analysis — All Modules
4 Papers Reviewed
Strengths Identified
  • Reduces manual paperwork across student-employer-institution workflows significantly
  • Centralizes internship tracking with real-time coordination among all stakeholders
  • Supports digital transformation in government education systems of Rajasthan
  • Improves administrative transparency and efficiency in higher education institutions
  • Data-driven placement decision support helps identify students needing training
  • Simplifies internship discovery, application tracking, and employer interaction
  • Provides monitoring dashboards for institutions to track student outcomes
  • Encourages centralized digital platforms and reduces siloed operations
Challenges & Risks
  • Limited scalability — mostly conceptual with fewer large-scale real-world implementations
  • Digital divide in rural Rajasthan limits equitable access across all districts
  • Requires significant training and infrastructure development in remote areas
  • ML placement models need large datasets; raises data privacy and ethical concerns
  • Limited pilot testing of InternConnect; strong security mechanisms still required
  • Employer verification protocols not yet standardized across the platform
  • Automated academic scoring may introduce bias against certain student groups
  • Sustained funding and political will needed for long-term infrastructure rollout

Policy Recommendations — Government of Rajasthan

  • Phase-wise rollout: begin with urban districts, expand only after rural connectivity is secured
  • Partner with NIC and REIL for infrastructure, cybersecurity, and data compliance
  • Mandate employer verification and establish district-level grievance redressal cells
  • Commission independent scalability audit before statewide IMS deployment
  • Appoint Data Protection Officer per DPDP Act 2023 compliance before ML deployment
